# Illustration of InSight Cruise Stage Separation * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA22xxx/PIA22828_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA22828.jpg) (2536 x 1418) (132.7 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA22828.tif) (2536 x 1418) (6.8 MB)

Caption:

This illustration shows NASA's InSight lander separating from its cruise stage as it prepares to enter Mars' atmosphere. The InSight lander is on the right, tucked inside a protective heat shield and back shell. The cruise stage with solar panels is on the left.

Background Info:

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, a division of Caltech in Pasadena, California, manages the InSight Project for NASA's Science Mission Directorate, Washington. Lockheed Martin Space, Denver, Colorado built the spacecraft. InSight is part of NASA's Discovery Program, which is managed by NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in Huntsville, Alabama.

For more information about the mission, go to https://mars.nasa.gov/insight .
