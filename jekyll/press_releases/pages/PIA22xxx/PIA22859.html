# Relatively Young * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA22xxx/PIA22859_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA22859.jpg) (1314 x 2677) (261.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA22859.tif) (1314 x 2677) (2.4 MB)

Caption:


Context image

This VIS image shows half of an unnamed crater in Noachis Terra. The complex rim and rough floor are indicative of a relatively young crater. With time and erosion, craters fill in with sediment, creating flatter floors and smoothing the overall topography.
