# Pre-Launch T-VAC Testing on InSight * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA22xxx/PIA22740_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA22740.jpg) (5760 x 3840) (1.5 MB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA22740.tif) (5760 x 3840) (43.7 MB)

Caption:

Engineers at Lockheed Martin Space, Denver, Colorado, prepare NASA's InSight lander for testing in a thermal vacuum chamber several months before launch.

Background Info:

JPL, a division of Caltech in Pasadena, California, manages the InSight Project for NASA's Science Mission Directorate, Washington. Lockheed Martin Space built and tested the spacecraft, including its cruise stage and lander, and supports spacecraft operations for the mission. InSight is part of NASA s Discovery Program, which is managed by NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in Huntsville, Alabama.

For more information about the mission, go to https://mars.nasa.gov/insight .
