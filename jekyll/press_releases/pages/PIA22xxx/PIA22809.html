# InSight on Its Parachute (Illustration) * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA22xxx/PIA22809_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA22809.jpg) (2754 x 1551) (112.2 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA22809.tif) (2754 x 1551) (5.9 MB)

Caption:

This illustration shows a simulated view of NASA's InSight lander descending on its parachute toward the surface of Mars.

Background Info:

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, a division of Caltech in Pasadena, California, manages the InSight Project for NASA's Science Mission Directorate, Washington. Lockheed Martin Space, Denver, Colorado built the spacecraft. InSight is part of NASA's Discovery Program, which is managed by NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in Huntsville, Alabama.

For more information about the mission, go to https://mars.nasa.gov/insight .
