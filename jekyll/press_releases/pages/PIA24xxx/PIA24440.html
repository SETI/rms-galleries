# Ingenuity Mars Helicopter Pilot's Logbook * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA24xxx/PIA24440_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA24440.jpg) (8256 x 5504) (11.1 MB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA24440.tif) (8256 x 5504) (121.5 MB)

Caption:

This image of the official pilot's logbook for the Ingenuity Mars Helicopter flights — the "Nominal Pilot's Logbook for Planets and Moons" — was taken at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Southern California on April 19, 2021, the day of Ingenuity's first historic flight. Pilot logbooks are used by aviators to provide a record of their flights, including current and accumulated flight time, number and locations of takeoffs and landings, as well as unique operating conditions and certifications.
