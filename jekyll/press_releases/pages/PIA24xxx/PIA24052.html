# Ariadnes Colles - False Color * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA24xxx/PIA24052_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA24052.jpg) (672 x 1423) (105.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA24052.tif) (672 x 1423) (1.9 MB)

Caption:


Context image

The THEMIS VIS camera contains 5 filters. The data from different filters can be combined in multiple ways to create a false color image. These false color images may reveal subtle variations of the surface not easily identified in a single band image. Today's false color image shows part of Ariadnes Colles. The term colles means hills or knobs. In this false color combination the hills stand out against the darker surrounding plains. This difference is due to the amount of dust covering the hills versus the plains.
