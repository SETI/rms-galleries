# Rabe Crater Dunes * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA24xxx/PIA24117_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA24117.jpg) (1325 x 2681) (189.1 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA24117.tif) (1325 x 2681) (2.2 MB)

Caption:


Context image
The large sand sheet with surface dune forms seen in this VIS image is located on the complex floor of Rabe Crater. The sand is likely derived by erosion into the deposit that fills most of the crater floor, creating a pit which hosts the dunes. This crater morphology is unique to Rabe Crater. Rabe Crater is located in Noachis Terra and is 108km in diameter (67 miles).
