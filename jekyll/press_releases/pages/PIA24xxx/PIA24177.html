# Components of MOXIE (Illustration) * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA24xxx/PIA24177_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA24177.jpg) (2000 x 1125) (122.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA24177.tif) (2000 x 1125) (2.4 MB)

Caption:

An illustration of MOXIE (Mars Oxygen ISRU Experiment) and its components. An air pump pulls in carbon dioxide gas from the Martian atmosphere, which is then regulated and fed to the Solid OXide Electrolyzer (SOXE), where it is electrochemically split to produce pure oxygen.

Background Info:

The Jet Propulsion Laboratory, which is managed for NASA by Caltech in Pasadena, California, built and manages operations of the Perseverance and Curiosity rovers.
