# Dawn Stereo Anaglyph of Impact Melt Deposits at Occator Crater, Ceres * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA24xxx/PIA24064_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA24064.jpg) (1234 x 1065) (116.3 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA24064.tif) (1234 x 1065) (3.0 MB)

Caption:

The Dawn spacecraft captured these stereo images of Occator crater on the dwarf planet Ceres in 2018. Framing camera images were used to construct this anaglyph view (which requires red-blue stereo glasses for viewing) of part of the northeastern rim of the crater. This area is approximately 4 miles (7 kilometers) wide and features a thin mantling layer of impact melt draped over faulted terrace blocks. Impact melt flowed through a gap in the blocks in the center of the frame. The spatial resolution of the stereo images is about 11 feet (3.5 meters) per pixel. Occator Crater, named after the Roman god of the agricultural practice of harrowing, is about 57 miles (92 kilometers) in diameter.

This image was produced by Dr. Paul Schenk at the Lunar and Planetary Institute in Houston.
