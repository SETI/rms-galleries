# A Microphone for Mars * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA24xxx/PIA24046_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA24046.jpg) (3024 x 4032) (1.2 MB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA24046.tif) (3024 x 4032) (30.3 MB)

Caption:

Components are visible on the port side of the Perseverance rover in this close-up image taken on Nov. 16, 2019, in High Bay 1 of the Spacecraft Assembly Facility at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Southern California. At center of the image, attached to the side of the rover, is a black cable bracket (with gold cabling running through it). Attached to the top of this black bracket — and gray in color — is the Entry Descent and Landing (EDL) microphone. Below the cable bracket are the Mars Oxygen In-Situ Resource Utilization Experiment (MOXIE) wall ports. The orange cable passing over it is part of ground support equipment.
