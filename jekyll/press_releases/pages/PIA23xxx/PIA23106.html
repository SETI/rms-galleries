# A Crater on the South Polar Layered Deposits * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23106_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23106.jpg) (2880 x 1800) (1.4 MB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23106.tif) (2880 x 1800) (15.6 MB)

This image is part of a campaign to image potential impact craters in the south polar layered deposits (ice cap). This feature looks like a strong candidate for an impact crater because it is very circular are still has a raised rim.

The sizes and densities of impact craters provide an estimate for the age of the landscape, which in turn provides a minimum age for the icy layers.

The map is projected here at a scale of 25 centimeters (9.8 inches) per pixel. (The original image scale is 24.8 centimeters [9.8 inches] per pixel [with 1 x 1 binning]; objects on the order of 75 centimeters [29.5 inches] across are resolved.) North is up.
