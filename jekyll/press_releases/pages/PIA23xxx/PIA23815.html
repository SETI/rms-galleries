# Tempe Fossae * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23815_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23815.jpg) (1416 x 2646) (391.8 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23815.tif) (1416 x 2646) (2.7 MB)

Caption:


Context image

Today's VIS image is shows a small portion of Tempe Fossae. The fossae are graben comprised of paired, parallel fractures with a down-dropped block of material between the fracture set. This morphology is created by extensional tectonic stresses. This image is located in a region of Tempe Terra that is complexly fractured. The complete fossae system in almost 2000 km (1242 miles) long.
