# McLaughlin Crater - False Color * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23108_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23108.jpg) (613 x 2704) (128.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23108.tif) (613 x 2704) (2.3 MB)

Caption:


Context image

The large crater at the bottom of today's image is called McLaughlin Crater. It is located west of Mawrth Vallis, on the margin between Arabia Terra and Chryse Planitia.

The THEMIS VIS camera contains 5 filters. The data from different filters can be combined in multiple ways to create a false color image. These false color images may reveal subtle variations of the surface not easily identified in a single band image.
