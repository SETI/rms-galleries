# Meridiani Planum * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23930_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23930.jpg) (1369 x 2641) (349.1 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23930.tif) (1369 x 2641) (2.6 MB)

Caption:


Context image

This VIS image shows layering of surface materials in Meridiani Planum. TES (Thermal Emission Spectrometer) initially detected hematite in a surface layer, which was confimed by THEMIS (THrmal EMision Imaging System). These findings supported a water rich origin of the hematite and led to the selection of the site for the Opportunity MER (Mars Exploration Rover).
