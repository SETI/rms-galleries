# Aerogel Greenhouses for Mars? * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23343_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23343.jpg) (4138 x 3991) (2.7 MB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23343.tif) (4138 x 3991) (48.4 MB)

Caption:

Scientists are exploring how aerogel, a translucent, Styrofoam-like material, could be used as a building material on Mars. Aerogel retains heat; structures built with it could raise temperatures enough to melt water ice on the Martian surface.
