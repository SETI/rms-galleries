# Crater Dunes - False Color * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23221_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23221.jpg) (652 x 1536) (55.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23221.tif) (652 x 1536) (2.2 MB)

Caption:


Context image

Today's false color image shows an unnamed crater in Acidalia Planitia. The dark blue feature on the crater floor is a mound of sand. The sand is tall enough to cast a shadow, with the sun is coming from the left (west). The texture on the surface of the sand are dune features created by wind action.

The THEMIS VIS camera contains 5 filters. The data from different filters can be combined in multiple ways to create a false color image. These false color images may reveal subtle variations of the surface not easily identified in a single band image.
