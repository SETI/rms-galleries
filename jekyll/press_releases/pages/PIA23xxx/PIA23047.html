# Curiosity on the Clay Unit * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23047_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23047.jpg) (1328 x 1184) (175.0 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23047.tif) (1328 x 1184) (4.4 MB)

Caption:

NASA's Curiosity Mars rover took this image with its Mast Camera (Mastcam) on Feb. 10, 2019 (Sol 2316). The rover is currently exploring a region of Mount Sharp nicknamed "Glen Torridon" that has lots of clay minerals.

Background Info:

Malin Space Science Systems, San Diego, built and operates the Mastcam. NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, a division of the Caltech in Pasadena, California, manages the Mars Science Laboratory Project for NASA's Science Mission Directorate, Washington. JPL designed and built the project's Curiosity rover.

More information about Curiosity is online at http://www.nasa.gov/msl and http://mars.jpl.nasa.gov/msl/ .
