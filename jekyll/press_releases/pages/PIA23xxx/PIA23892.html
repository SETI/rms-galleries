# Science on the Horizon * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23892_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23892.jpg) (3840 x 2160) (507.3 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23892.tif) (3840 x 2160) (20.6 MB)

Caption:

Michael Tuite and Rachel Kronyak of NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory in a dry lakebed in the Nevada desert. During the February 2020 exercise, they served as part of a field team that stood in as Mars a rover, receiving commands to seek out images and other data for review by scientists who will work with NASA's Perseverance Mars rover.
