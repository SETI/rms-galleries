# Dusty's Panorama * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23247_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23247.jpg) (7620 x 2591) (2.0 MB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23247.tif) (7620 x 2591) (15.4 MB)

Caption:

This 360-degree panorama was taken by "Dusty," a fully-working replica of NASA's Opportunity rover at the agency's Jet Propulsion Laboratory. The panorama was taken as part of a software test. Members of the Opportunity team gathered to sit in during the panorama.

The panorama was taken by Dusty's Panoramic Camera, or Pancam, on Sept. 6, 2018.

Background Info:

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, a division of Caltech in Pasadena, California, manages the Mars Exploration Rover Project for NASA's Science Mission Directorate in Washington.

For more information about Opportunity, visit https://www.nasa.gov/rovers and https://mars.nasa.gov/mer .
