# Jupiter's South Equatorial Region * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23595_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23595.jpg) (3942 x 1580) (838.0 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23595.tif) (3942 x 1580) (16.3 MB)

Caption:

The JunoCam imager aboard NASA's Juno spacecraft captured this image of Jupiter's southern equatorial region on Sept. 1, 2017. The image is oriented so Jupiter's poles (not visible) run left-to-right of frame.

Background Info:

JunoCam's raw images are available for the public to peruse and process into image products at
https://missionjuno.swri.edu/junocam/processing .

More information about Juno is at https://www.nasa.gov/juno and https://missionjuno.swri.edu .
