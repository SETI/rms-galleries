# What is a Brown Dwarf? * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23685_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23685.jpg) (4800 x 2700) (523.7 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23685.tif) (4800 x 2700) (11.4 MB)

Caption:

Brown dwarfs are more massive than planets but not quite as massive as stars. Generally speaking, they have between 13 and 80 times the mass of Jupiter. A brown dwarf becomes a star if its core pressure gets high enough to start nuclear fusion.
