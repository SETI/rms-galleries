# Curiosity's Selfie at Aberlady and Kilmarie * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA23xxx/PIA23240_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA23240.jpg) (9990 x 12161) (9.8 MB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA23240.tif) (9990 x 12161) (275.7 MB)

Caption:

NASA's Curiosity Mars rover took this selfie on May 12, 2019 (the 2,405th Martian day, or sol, of the mission). To the lower-left of the rover are its two recent drill holes, at targets called "Aberlady" and "Kilmarie."

These are Curiosity's 20th and 21st drill sites.

The selfie is composed of 57 individual images taken by the rover's Mars Hand Lens Imager (MAHLI), a camera on the end of the rover's robotic arm. The images are stitched together into a panorama, and the robotic arm is digitally removed.

Background Info:

MAHLI was built by Malin Space Science Systems in San Diego. NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, a division of Caltech in Pasadena, manages the Mars Science Laboratory Project for the NASA Science Mission Directorate in Washington. JPL designed and built the project's Curiosity rover.

More information about Curiosity is at http://www.nasa.gov/msl and http://mars.jpl.nasa.gov/msl/ .
